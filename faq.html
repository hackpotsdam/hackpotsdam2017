You've got questions

We've got answers

Where is it?

Clarkson University. 8 Clarkson Ave, Potsdam, NY 13699

What building will it be in?

The MPR's. We'll be posting how to get there soon!

Where should I park?

Stay tuned! We'll let you know.

Will there be a theme?

Nope! People can build whatever they would like with whatever tools they desire!

Who can participate?

The hackathon is open to all college students, professors and alumni. However, only currently attending college students are eligible for prizes.

Are there any rules?

No code can be written until the event kicks off. Libraries and frameworks are allowed.

I have an idea! How can I share it?

You’ll have a chance to share it the day of the event. We expect the teams to form organically.

How can I submit and present my project?

We are currently looking into options for submitting projects, but everyone will have the opportunity to showcase their projects on the projector on Sunday.

How do I join a team? Can I be on more than one team?

Talk with people about their ideas and find which interests you the most! You can be on multiple teams if you would like, but we encourage you to just be on one team.

Do I have to attend the full 24 hours?

We recommend you stay for a majority of the time and encourage you to come and participate as much as you can.

How many people per team?

Having too many people on one team means that not everyone may have the opportunity to contribute, and having too few people on a team makes it harder to build something. The decision is yours.

Do I need to bring anything?

Please bring a valid college ID (if you're an alumni, please let us know beforehand on the sign up sheet), laptop, charger, and eagerness to build something awesome!

Can I use hardware like raspberry pis, arduino boards, etc?

Of course!

Are laptops provided?

We cannot provide anyone laptops, however the ITL will be open with multiple computers (around 10) for people to use.

Can I come to watch & hang out rather than participate?

Yes, if you would like to just stop by to hang out and learn about the hackathon you are more than welcome to. Again, we encourage you to participate

I don’t think I know enough to participate.

As long as you have interest and passion for a project - there will be a way for you to make an impact. Trust us, some people come not knowing anything about the tools / technologies they using and walk away with a ton of knowledge gained.

Can I sleep at the event?

If you don’t mind uncomfortable sleeping spots, sure. The doors will be locking at some point, and we'll update this when we get an exact time. If you can stay up for all 24 hours though, you are indeed a trooper.

I'm not a developer, how can I contribute?

or more information on how non-developers can contribute to a hackathon, please explore these two links. 5 hackathon roles that have nothing to do with coding and hackathons aren't just for developers.

I still have a question! Who do I contact?

Please contact the 2016 SUNY Potsdam ACM president at peplinjj199@potsdam.edu if you have any questions or concerns.

Our Purpose

SUNY Potsdam's ACM and Clarkson's COSI both connect students with a passion for technology. Through hackathons, this innovation and cross-collaboration between schools is fueled.
