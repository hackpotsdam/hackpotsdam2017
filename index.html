What is Hack Potsdam?

hack·a·thon (noun) -- an event in which computer programmers, hardware engineers and others involved in product development, including graphic designers, interface designers and project managers, collaborate intensively on software and hardware projects over a short period of time.

Share Ideas

Bring an idea that you have and form a team or go solo. You'll be able to pitch your idea to everyone.

Form Teams

Teams form organically around the ideas that are shared. Other than what type of pizza to eat, this is one of the more challenging decisions.

Build Projects

You'll be surprised what you can create in just 24 hours. Walk away with a great expereince and something you can show off.

These aren't the only reasons to go to a hackathon. People also go to learn new technologies, meet new people, eat lots of pizza, build a product, change the world, join a team, lead a team, win prizes, or get hired.

Hack Potsdam, Spring 2017

The SUNY Potsdam ACM and Clarkson COSI host weekend hackathons that are open to ALL students and alumni (we require a valid student ID). We host it at SUNY Potsdam in the Fall and Clarkson University in the Spring. Hack Potsdam is FREE to all attendees. Oh yeah, and lot's of free food. Come and join us!

Our Story

Here's how this event all got started.

  • Fall 2016

    First Hack Potsdam

    After the ACM President, Jesse, had been Hack Upstate, he had the crazy idea to help bring one to Potsdam. With the tremendous help of the e-board members and a lot of other people I can't fit on this line to thank, that crazy idea came to life.

  • Spring 2017

    The Collaboration Continues

    With the wild success of the first hackathon, SUNY Potsdam's ACM and Clarkson's COSI decide to collaborate on the spring iteration of the event.
